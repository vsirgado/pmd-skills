# Google Ranking Factors — Healthcare & Medical Prioritized

## Tier 1: Non-Negotiable (Fix These First)

These factors can prevent ranking entirely if they fail.

| Factor | What to Check | Pass Criteria |
|--------|--------------|---------------|
| Indexation | Page is in Google index | `site:url` returns result |
| HTTPS | Valid SSL, no mixed content | Browser shows padlock, no warnings |
| Mobile-friendly | Passes Google's test | No mobile usability errors in GSC |
| Crawlability | Googlebot can access the page | Not blocked in robots.txt or noindex |
| Content exists | Sufficient text on page | 300+ words minimum for any indexed page |
| No manual penalties | Google Search Console | No manual actions reported |

## Tier 2: Core Ranking Drivers (Highest Impact)

### Content Quality & Relevance
**Weight: Very High**
- Content directly answers the searcher's query
- Comprehensive coverage of the topic (covers subtopics competitors cover)
- Unique insights, data, or expert perspective
- Regularly updated (especially for medical content where guidelines change)
- Zero factual errors (especially critical for YMYL)

**Healthcare Specific:**
- Content reviewed and approved by licensed medical professional
- Aligned with current clinical guidelines and evidence-based medicine
- No contradictory or outdated medical claims

### E-E-A-T Signals
**Weight: Very High for YMYL**

**Experience signals:**
- First-person clinical experience described
- Real patient outcomes or case examples
- Procedural details only a practitioner would know

**Expertise signals:**
- Author has relevant credentials (MD, DO, RN, NP, PA, PharmD, etc.)
- Author bio links to license verification
- Content depth demonstrates clinical knowledge

**Authoritativeness signals:**
- Site linked to by other medical authorities
- Physician listed in medical directories
- Practice mentioned in local news or medical publications

**Trustworthiness signals:**
- Transparent authorship and affiliations
- Clear contact information and physical address
- Citations to primary sources
- Privacy policy, terms, medical disclaimer present
- No deceptive design patterns

### Backlink Authority
**Weight: High**
- Total referring domains (more unique domains = stronger signal)
- Domain authority of linking sites (medical directories >> random blogs)
- Relevance of linking site (medical + local >> unrelated)
- Link placement (in-content >> footer/sidebar)
- Anchor text (branded/natural >> exact-match)

**High-Value Link Sources for Healthcare:**
- Healthgrades, Vitals, Zocdoc, Doximity, WebMD Find a Doctor
- Hospital/health system affiliations
- Local Chamber of Commerce or business directories
- Medical association member pages
- Local news coverage (especially for practices)
- .edu and .gov references
- Patient advocacy organization mentions

## Tier 3: On-Page Signals (Strong Impact)

| Factor | Best Practice | Impact |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| Title tag | Primary keyword first, 50-60 chars | High |
| H1 | Contains primary keyword, unique | High |
| Meta description | 150-160 chars, compelling CTA | Medium (CTR) |
| Keyword in URL | Clean, descriptive URL slug | Medium |
| Content length | Matches or exceeds top competitors | High |
| Semantic coverage | LSI keywords, related topics covered | High |
| Internal links received | Linked from relevant pages on same site | Medium-High |
| Image alt text | Descriptive, includes keywords | Low-Medium |
| Structured data | Correct schema implementation | Medium-High |
| Page depth | Accessible within 3 clicks from homepage | Medium |

## Tier 5: Local SEO Factors (Critical for Practice Pages)

**Weight: Very High for local pack rankings**

### Google Business Profile (GBP) Signals
- Claimed and verified GBP listing
- Business name matches website exactly
- Address matches exactly (NAP consistency)
- Primary category: correct medical specialty
- Secondary categories: all applicable specialties listed
- Hours of operation accurate and updated
- Photos: exterior, interior, staff, equipment (20+ photos minimum)
- Posts: regular updates (at least monthly)
- Reviews: volume AND recency AND responses
- Q&A section answered by practice

### Citation Consistency (NAP)
Name, Address, Phone must be **identical** across:
- Google Business Profile
- Website (footer and contact page)
- Yelp
- Facebook
- Healthgrades
- Vitals
- Bing Places
- Apple Maps
- Foursquare
- All medical directories

**Even minor inconsistencies (Suite vs. Ste., LLC vs. no LLC) dilute local authority.**

### Local Review Strategy
- Volume: More reviews than local competitors
- Recency: At least 2-3 new reviews per month
- Diversity: Google + Healthgrades + Yelp + Zocdoc
- Response rate: Respond to 100% of reviews within 48 hours
- Rating: Maintain 4.5+ stars average

### Local Content Signals
- City/neighborhood name in title, H1, and body text
- "Serving [city], [nearby city], [region]" in footer or service area section
- Schema geo coordinates for practice location
- Embed Google Maps on contact page

## Tier 6: User Engagement Signals (Indirect Impact)

Google uses click data and engagement as quality signals.

| Signal | What it Means | How to Improve |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| Click-through rate (CTR) | % of impressions that result in a click | Better title tags and meta descriptions |
| Pogo-sticking | User clicks result, returns to SERP immediately | Match content to search intent |
| Dwell time | Time spent on page before returning to SERP | Longer, more engaging content |
| Pages per session | User visits multiple pages | Strong internal linking + CTAs |

## Healthcare-Specific Ranking Landmines

These factors disproportionately hurt medical sites:

1. **Outdated medical information** — Google's quality raters flag this heavily
2. **No author credentials** — anonymous health content gets downgraded
3. **Excessive ads or popups** — especially above the fold
4. **Broken links to cited sources** — hurts trust signals
5. **Duplicate location pages** — thin, templated pages for each city without unique content
6. **Overpromising outcomes** — "guaranteed results" or "cure" language triggers quality flags
7. **Keyword-stuffed content** — especially in meta tags and headers
8. **Purchased links** — high-risk for Google penalties, especially spammy health directories
