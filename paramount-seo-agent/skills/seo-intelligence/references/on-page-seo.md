# On-Page SEO Reference — Optimization Formulas & Templates

## Title Tag Formulas

### Service Page Formula
```
[Primary Keyword] in [City] | [Practice Name]
```
Example: `Knee Replacement Surgery in Miami | South Florida Orthopedics`

### Doctor/Physician Page Formula
```
Dr. [Name], [Specialty] — [City], [State]
```
Example: `Dr. Maria Chen, MD — Dermatologist in Austin, TX`

### Blog/Informational Formula
```
[Target Keyword]: [Benefit or Qualifier] | [Brand]
```
Example: `Botox vs. Fillers: Which Is Right for You? | Revive MedSpa`

### Rules
- 50-60 characters (Google truncates at ~580px width)
- Primary keyword within first 3 words whenever possible
- Each page has a unique title tag — no duplicates
- Never keyword-stuff — one primary keyword, natural language
- Include location for local service pages

## Meta Description Formulas

### Service Page Formula
```
[Benefit statement]. [What you do] in [Location]. [CTA].
```
Example: `Relieve chronic knee pain with minimally invasive surgery. Board-certified orthopedic surgeons serving Miami. Schedule your free consultation today.`

### Blog Post Formula
```
[Hook/question]. [What the article covers]. [Who it's for].
```
Example: `Wondering if Botox is right for you? This guide covers costs, results, and what to expect — written by a board-certified dermatologist.`

### Rules
- 150-160 characters
- Include primary keyword naturally
- Include a call to action
- Make it compelling — this is your ad copy in the SERP
- Unique per page

## Heading Structure Templates

### Service Page H-Tag Hierarchy
```
H1: [Primary Keyword + Location] (e.g., "Botox in Miami, FL")
  H2: What Is [Service]?
  H2: Benefits of [Service]
    H3: [Benefit 1]
    H3: [Benefit 2]
  H2: How [Service] Works
    H3: Step 1
    H3: Step 2
  H2: Who Is a Candidate for [Service]?
  H2: What to Expect During Your [Service] Appointment
  H2: Frequently Asked Questions About [Service]
  H2: Schedule Your [Service] Consultation in [City]
```

### Blog Post H-Tag Hierarchy
```
H1: [Exact Target Keyword or Close Variant]
  H2: [Key Subtopic 1]
    H3: [Supporting point]
  H2: [Key Subtopic 2]
  H2: [Comparison or Alternative if applicable]
  H2: [Expert Takeaway / Recommendation]
  H2: Frequently Asked Questions
```

### Rules
- One H1 per page — never more
- H1 must contain primary keyword
- H2s should contain secondary/LSI keywords
- Logical hierarchy: never skip from H2 to H4
- Headers should be readable out of context (screen readers, featured snippets)

## Content Optimization Checklist

### Keyword Placement
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 100 words of body text
- [ ] Primary keyword in H1
- [ ] Primary keyword in at least one H2
- [ ] Primary keyword in meta title and meta description
- [ ] Primary keyword in at least one image alt tag
- [ ] Primary keyword 1-2% density (not stuffed)
- [ ] LSI/semantic variants naturally distributed throughout

### Content Quality (YMYL/Healthcare Standards)
- [ ] Content written or reviewed by a licensed medical professional
- [ ] Author byline with credentials (MD, DO, RN, etc.)
- [ ] Date published and date last reviewed/updated visible
- [ ] Medical claims are cited with sources (PubMed, CDC, Mayo Clinic, ACOG, etc.)
- [ ] No outdated medical information
- [ ] Balanced, evidence-based information
- [ ] Clear disclaimers where appropriate (not a substitute for professional medical advice)

### Content Length Guidelines
| Page Type | Minimum Words | Optimal Range |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| Home page | 500 | 800-1,200 |
| Service/procedure page | 800 | 1,200-2,000 |
| Location page | 400 | 600-1,000 |
| Blog/article (informational) | 1,000 | 1,500-2,500 |
| FAQ page | 300 | 500-1,000 |
| About/physician bio page | 400 | 600-1,200 |
| Condition page | 1,000 | 1,500-3,000 |

### Internal Linking Strategy
- [ ] Every page has at least 3 internal links TO other pages
- [ ] Every page receives at least 1 internal link FROM another page (no orphans)
- [ ] Anchor text is descriptive (not "click here" or "read more")
- [ ] Pillar page links to all cluster pages
- [ ] Cluster pages link back to pillar page
- [ ] Related service pages link to each other
- [ ] Blog posts link to relevant service pages (conversion internal links)

### Image Optimization
- [ ] Every image has a descriptive alt tag containing relevant keywords
- [ ] Alt tags describe what's in the image (for accessibility)
- [ ] File names are descriptive: `botox-treatment-miami.webp` not `IMG_001.jpg`
- [ ] Images are compressed under 200KB
- [ ] Images served in WebP format
- [ ] Hero images have explicit width and height attributes

## LSI Keyword Research Process

1. Search target keyword in Google
2. Scroll to "People Also Ask" — these are related intent keywords
3. Scroll to "Related Searches" — these are semantic extensions
4. Review top 3 ranking pages — what subheadings do they use?
5. Use Google Autocomplete — type keyword + alphabet letters
6. Check Google Search Console for queries you already rank for

### Healthcare LSI Examples
**Primary**: "knee replacement surgery"
**LSI**: total knee replacement, knee arthroplasty, recovery time, cost, eligibility, risks, partial knee replacement, minimally invasive

**Primary**: "Botox injections"
**LSI**: botulinum toxin, wrinkle treatment, units needed, how long it lasts, side effects, cost per unit, lip flip, masseter Botox

## E-E-A-T Optimization Checklist

### Experience
- [ ] Content includes first-hand patient cases or clinical experience
- [ ] Real before/after results mentioned (with permission/compliance)
- [ ] "In my [X] years of practice..." or similar experience signals

### Expertise
- [ ] Medical professional author or co-author
- [ ] Author bio page links to: medical license verification, board certification, CV/resume
- [ ] Content demonstrates deep medical knowledge, not generic info

### Authoritativeness
- [ ] Cited by other websites (backlinks from medical directories, news, .edu)
- [ ] Doctor/practice listed on: Healthgrades, Doximity, Vitals, WebMD, ZocDoc
- [ ] Press mentions or media coverage
- [ ] Speaking engagements, publications, research cited

### Trustworthiness
- [ ] About page is detailed and specific (real people, real credentials)
- [ ] Contact page with physical address and phone number
- [ ] Privacy policy and terms of service pages exist
- [ ] HTTPS sitewide
- [ ] Reviews visible on site or linked to Google/Healthgrades reviews
- [ ] No deceptive claims or misleading information
