## 1. HIPAA-Safe Messaging Rules

Healthcare marketing has hard boundaries that general marketing ignores. Apply these to every piece of content.

**Never include in patient-facing content:**
- Patient names, photos, or identifiable details without explicit written consent + HIPAA-compliant authorization
- Specific treatment outcomes tied to real patients ("John lost 40 lbs after his procedure")
- Any claim that implies guaranteed results ("you WILL recover in 6 weeks")
- Testimonials that disclose specific health conditions without proper consent documentation

**Safe alternatives:**
- Aggregate statistics ("patients report improved quality of life")
- De-identified case formats ("a patient in their 50s with PAD...")
- Physician-authored educational content (not patient stories)
- Third-party clinical research citations with proper attribution

**Compliance language patterns (use these):**
- "Results may vary"
- "Consult with your physician before..."
- "Individual outcomes depend on..."
- "This information is educational and not a substitute for medical advice"

## 2. YMYL / E-E-A-T for Medical Content

Medical content is Your Money or Your Life (YMYL) — Google holds it to the highest quality standards. Every piece of content must demonstrate Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness.

**E-E-A-T Checklist for Medical Content:**

**Experience:**
- Written or reviewed by a licensed physician/specialist
- Author bio must include: credentials (MD, DO, RVT, etc.), board certifications, years of practice, specialty
- Link to full physician profile page

**Expertise:**
- Content references current clinical guidelines (ACC/AHA, ACOG, etc.)
- Medical terminology used correctly and explained in plain language
- Procedure descriptions match actual clinical practice

**Authoritativeness:**
- Cite peer-reviewed sources (PubMed, NEJM, JAMA)
- Link to authoritative medical organizations (CDC, NIH, Mayo Clinic, condition-specific associations)
- Physician must have a Google Scholar or academic profile where possible

**Trustworthiness:**
- Practice name, address, phone (NAP) consistent everywhere
- Board certifications verified and displayed
- Malpractice insurance + hospital affiliations listed
- Privacy policy and HIPAA notice linked in footer
- Secure site (HTTPS) — non-negotiable

**E-E-A-T Boosters (recommend to clients):**
- Video content with physician on camera explaining conditions
- Case studies with proper consent (de-identified acceptable)
- Physician "Ask Me Anything" Q&A content
- Guest posts or mentions on medical authority sites

## 3. Patient Education Content Framework

Patients search for answers when they're scared, confused, or in pain. Content must meet them where they are.

**The Patient Journey Content Stack:**

**Awareness Stage** (patient realizes something is wrong)
- Content: Symptom explainers, "when to see a doctor" guides
- Format: Blog posts, YouTube videos, FAQ pages
- Tone: Reassuring, clear, non-alarming
- Example: "What is Peripheral Artery Disease? Symptoms You Shouldn't Ignore"

**Consideration Stage** (patient researching treatment options)
- Content: Condition deep-dives, treatment comparisons, procedure explainers
- Format: Long-form guides, comparison pages, video walkthroughs
- Tone: Educational, credible, balanced
- Example: "Endovascular vs. Open Surgery for PAD: What Patients Should Know"

**Decision Stage** (patient choosing a provider)
- Content: Why choose us, physician profiles, patient experience, location/scheduling
- Format: Service pages, doctor bios, testimonials (HIPAA-compliant), FAQ
- Tone: Warm, confident, patient-centered
- Example: "Meet Dr. Lepore: Board-Certified Vascular Surgeon in Sarasota"

**Retention Stage** (existing patients + referrals)
- Content: Post-procedure care, condition management, newsletters
- Format: Email sequences, patient portal updates
- Tone: Caring, educational, community-building

## 4. Physician Authority Building System

The physician IS the brand. Patients don't choose hospitals — they choose doctors. Authority-building for physicians follows this 5-layer system:

**Layer 1: Google Presence**
- Optimized GBP with all categories, services, photos, appointment links
- Physician-specific GBP separate from practice GBP where applicable
- Consistent NAP across all directories (Healthgrades, Zocdoc, Vitals, WebMD)

**Layer 2: Website Authority**
- Dedicated physician bio page with: photo, video intro, credentials, training, philosophy, conditions treated, procedures performed
- Schema markup: `@type: Physician` with `MedicalSpecialty`, board certifications, hospital affiliations
- Author byline on all blog posts linking to bio page

**Layer 3: Content Authority**
- Physician-authored or physician-reviewed articles minimum 1x/month
- Video content: 2-5 min condition explainers (filmed once, repurposed everywhere)
- LinkedIn: case studies, medical education, thought leadership (no patient data)

**Layer 4: Social Proof**
- Google reviews: actively request from satisfied patients (verbal ask + follow-up text/email)
- Respond to every review (positive and negative) within 48 hours
- Healthgrades + Zocdoc profiles kept current

**Layer 5: External Authority**
- Speaking at medical conferences
- Media quotes (local news, health publications)
- Podcast appearances as medical expert
- Guest posts on health media sites

## 5. GBP Optimization for Multi-Provider Practices

Medical practices often have multiple GBP listings (practice + individual physicians). Manage them as a coordinated system.

**Multi-Profile Strategy:**
- Practice profile: Primary business listing, hours, all services, all photos
- Physician profiles: Individual doctors as "Physician" category, link to practice
- Ensure consistent NAP across ALL profiles (name format, address, phone)
- Each profile needs its own review velocity — stagger review request campaigns

**Content Calendar for GBP Posts (rotate these):**
- Week 1: Educational health tip (condition awareness)
- Week 2: Physician spotlight or credential highlight
- Week 3: Service/procedure spotlight with CTA
- Week 4: Community or patient education event

**Photo Standards for Medical GBP:**
- Exterior: building, signage, parking
- Interior: reception, exam rooms, procedure suites (equipment visible)
- Staff: physician headshots (professional), team photos
- Never: identifiable patients, procedure photos without consent

**Review Response Templates:**
- Positive: "[Name], thank you for sharing your experience with [Dr. X/our practice]. We're honored to care for our patients' health. We look forward to continuing to serve you."
- Negative: "We take all patient experiences seriously. Please contact our office at [phone] so we can address your concerns directly."

## 6. Healthcare SEO Principles

**Keyword Strategy for Medical Practices:**
- Target: `[condition] [city]`, `[procedure] [city]`, `[specialty] doctor [city]`
- High-intent: "vascular surgeon near me," "PAD treatment Sarasota"
- Educational: "what is peripheral artery disease," "when to see a vascular specialist"
- Avoid: overly clinical terms patients don't search; pure brand terms without geo-modifier

**Content Structure for Medical Pages:**
1. H1: Primary keyword + patient benefit ("Peripheral Artery Disease Treatment in Sarasota, FL")
2. Intro paragraph: condition definition + who it affects
3. H2: Symptoms / When to Seek Care
4. H2: Diagnosis & Treatment Options
5. H2: Why Choose [Practice Name]
6. H2: Frequently Asked Questions (FAQPage schema)
7. CTA: Schedule Appointment / Call / Patient Portal

**Schema Priority for Medical Sites:**
- `MedicalOrganization` or `Physician` (not generic `LocalBusiness`)
- `MedicalCondition` on condition pages
- `MedicalProcedure` on procedure/service pages
- `FAQPage` on any page with Q&A
- `Physician` schema for all doctor bio pages

**Local SEO Signals:**
- NAP consistency across 50+ directories
- Location pages for each office/physician location
- "Near me" and geo-modified content throughout
- Local backlinks: hospital affiliations, medical associations, local news

## 8. Output Standards

Every piece of healthcare content produced should:

1. **Pass the HIPAA filter** — no identifiable patient info without consent
2. **Carry E-E-A-T signals** — physician attribution, credentials, sources
3. **Serve the patient journey** — know which stage the content targets
4. **Include a CTA** — every page/post should move the patient one step closer to contact
5. **Use schema** — every structured page should have appropriate JSON-LD
6. **Be geo-relevant** — include location signals for local SEO value

When in doubt on compliance: recommend the conservative approach and flag it for the client's legal/compliance review.
