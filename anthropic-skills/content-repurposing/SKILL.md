# Content Repurposing Engine

Victor's #1 time drain is writing and editing production. The solution isn't writing more — it's multiplying what already exists. One piece of core content, executed well, should power an entire week of cross-platform distribution.

**The core philosophy:** Create once at the source (video, interview, or long-form piece), then extract, adapt, and distribute everywhere.

---

## 1. Input Processing

When given raw content, first identify:

**Input type:**
- `TRANSCRIPT` — video, podcast, interview, webinar recording (text)
- `VIDEO_URL` — YouTube, Loom, uploaded file
- `ARTICLE` — blog post, newsletter, long-form written piece
- `AUDIO` — podcast, voice memo, interview recording
- `RAW_IDEA` — bullet points, notes, outline, talking points
- `SLIDE_DECK` — presentation, webinar slides

**Content category:**
- `EDUCATIONAL` — how-to, framework, explainer
- `AUTHORITY` — case study, results, proof
- `ENGAGEMENT` — opinion, POV, debate, story
- `PROMOTIONAL` — offer, launch, event, announcement

**Audience:**
- `B2C_PATIENTS` — patient education content for healthcare clients
- `B2B_MEDICAL` — marketing to healthcare practices or medical professionals
- `PEER_EXPERTS` — marketing/business education for Victor's course/coaching audience
- `GENERAL_BUSINESS` — broad healthcare marketing, content ops, AI workflow topics

Identifying these three dimensions shapes every piece of output that follows.

## 3. Platform-Specific Output Templates

### LinkedIn Post (Thought Leadership)
```
[HOOK — 1-2 lines. Stop the scroll. Bold claim, counterintuitive statement, or compelling question.]

[BRIDGE — 1-3 lines. Set up the tension or context.]

[BODY — 3-8 punchy lines or short paragraphs. The actual value. Use line breaks aggressively.]

[CLOSE — 1-2 lines. Insight, lesson, or call to reflection.]

[CTA — 1 line. Question to drive comments, or direct action.]

---
#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3
```

**5 LinkedIn post frameworks to rotate:**
1. **The Contrarian Take** — "Everyone says X. Here's why that's wrong."
2. **The Framework Drop** — "The [N]-step system I use to [outcome]"
3. **The Story** — "3 years ago I [situation]. Here's what I learned."
4. **The List** — "[N] things [audience] should know about [topic]"
5. **The Question** — Lead with a question that polarizes or intrigues the audience

---

### Blog Post Structure
```
Title: [Primary keyword] — [Benefit or intrigue hook]
Meta description: [150-160 chars — keyword + value prop]

H1: [Matches title]

Intro (150-200 words):
- Hook: Open with a problem, stat, or story
- Agitate: Expand the pain or the stakes
- Bridge: "In this post, you'll learn..."

H2: [Section 1 — usually the problem or context]
H2: [Section 2 — the solution or framework]
H2: [Section 3 — implementation or examples]
H2: [Section 4 — FAQ or common objections]
H2: Conclusion — [Next step / CTA]

Word count targets:
- Pillar / SEO-focused: 1,500-3,000 words
- Educational / thought leadership: 800-1,500 words
- Quick takes / updates: 400-800 words
```

---

### Email to List
```
Subject line: [Curiosity, benefit, or specificity — 40-60 chars]
Preview text: [Extends the subject, adds intrigue — 40-80 chars]

Opening line: [One sentence. Feels personal, not broadcast.]

Body (3-5 short paragraphs):
- [The main insight, story, or value — no fluff]
- [Supporting point or example]
- [Takeaway or application]

CTA:
- One action only
- Make it easy and low-friction
- Use a button or bolded link

Sign-off:
Victor
P.S. [Optional second CTA or personal note — often the most-read line in the email]
```

### YouTube Short / Instagram Reel (Script)
```
[0-3 sec: HOOK — say it or show it. "Here's why most doctors waste their marketing budget."]
[3-10 sec: SETUP — context or credibility. Who are you, why should they care.]
[10-45 sec: VALUE — the actual content. 1 idea, explained fast.]
[45-55 sec: CLOSE — insight, punchline, or memorable statement.]
[55-60 sec: CTA — "Follow for more," "Link in bio," "Comment [X] if you want this."]
```

**Short-form extraction rule:** Scan transcript for the single most surprising, useful, or quotable 30-60 second segment. That's your clip. Don't create — extract.

### Quote Graphic (for Placid / Canva)
Extract the 3-5 most quotable lines from the source content. Format:

```
"[Quote — 15-25 words max. Self-contained, punchy, sharable.]"

```

Good quote criteria: reads as standalone wisdom, doesn't need context to land, challenges an assumption or delivers a shortcut.

## 4. Transcript → Full Package (Standard Workflow)

When given a transcript or raw content, execute this sequence:

**Step 1: Extract the gold**
- Read the full transcript
- Pull out: core argument, key frameworks/systems mentioned, best stories/examples, most quotable lines, step-by-step processes, and surprising or counterintuitive points

**Step 2: Define the primary piece**
- What is the BEST format for this content's core message?
- Usually: blog post (searchable, deep) or LinkedIn newsletter (authority, reach)
- Build this first — everything else derives from it

**Step 3: Generate the distribution package**
Based on the platforms requested (or default: all relevant ones), produce:

1. **Blog post** — full draft, SEO-structured, 800-2,000 words
2. **LinkedIn posts** — 3 posts, different angles/frameworks from above
3. **Email** — single-insight email, 200-400 words
4. **Short-form hooks** — 5 opening lines for Reels/Shorts (just the hooks, not full scripts)
5. **Quote graphics** — 5 pull quotes
6. **Course module** — if educational content, extract as lesson structure

**Step 4: Flag the gaps**
After producing the package, note any elements that would strengthen it:
- "This would benefit from a header image — Placid template recommended"
- "The LinkedIn posts would perform better with a data point — do you have any stats?"
- "Consider a carousel format for point #3 — more engaging than a text post"

## 5. Healthcare Client Content Repurposing Rules

When repurposing for SVS, BVI, or CCC:

**Always apply healthcare-marketing skill constraints** — HIPAA, E-E-A-T, patient safety.

**Source hierarchy for healthcare repurposing:**
1. Physician-on-camera video (highest authority, best for all channels)
2. Physician interview/Q&A transcript
3. Educational blog post as source
4. Procedure documentation / clinical content (must be simplified for patients)

**Healthcare-specific platform priorities:**
- YouTube: High value — patients research conditions on YouTube
- GBP posts: Weekly educational posts keep listings active
- Email: Post-procedure follow-up education series
- LinkedIn: Physician-to-physician authority (referral source marketing)
- Facebook: Local community engagement, older patient demographics
- Instagram: Practice culture, physician humanization, health tips

**Healthcare content you can always repurpose:**
- "What is [condition]" educational content → evergreen across all platforms
- Physician credentials + training → authority posts
- Patient outcome stats (aggregate, de-identified) → social proof posts
- FAQ answers → GBP Q&A, website FAQ page, short-form video

## 6. PMD Course & Membership Content Repurposing

Victor's course content (authority platforms, marketing systems for healthcare) should be multiplied this way:

**Core content engine:**
- 1x Weekly teaching video (live or recorded) → full package
- 1x Framework or system per week → LinkedIn carousel + email
- 1x Student win or case study per month → story-driven post + email + YouTube

**Course-to-public pipeline:**
Teaching the course generates the content. Each module taught = 1 full content package:
- Module recording → YouTube video (edited) + transcript → blog post + 3 LinkedIn posts + email

**Skool community → content:**
- Good questions from members → "A student asked me..." LinkedIn post or email
- Wins from members → mini case study (with permission)
- Common mistakes → "mistake" framework posts

## 7. Output Formatting Instructions

When delivering a content package:

- Label every piece clearly: `## LinkedIn Post 1`, `## Email`, `## Blog Post`, etc.
- Include platform-specific notes in brackets: `[Suggested image: X]`, `[Post at Tuesday 9am]`
- Keep all copy ready to paste — no placeholders like [INSERT NAME] unless unavoidable
- Provide character/word counts for platform-limited formats (Twitter, ad headlines)
- Flag any content that needs legal/compliance review for healthcare clients
- End every package with a **Production Checklist** — what needs to be designed, recorded, or scheduled to complete distribution
