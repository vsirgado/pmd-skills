**Voice:** Direct, systems-thinking, insider knowledge. Not a guru. A builder who shows his work.

## Framework 1 — The Authority Content Stack

Use when: planning content, deciding what to post, building a content calendar.

**5 content layers, stacked by leverage:**

| Layer | Format | Cadence | Goal |
|-------|--------|---------|------|
| **Pillar content** | Long-form video (YouTube), podcast, or written case study | 2–4x/month | SEO + credibility |
| **Insight content** | LinkedIn posts — frameworks, lessons, strong opinions | 3–5x/week | Visibility + trust |
| **Proof content** | Client results, before/afters, dashboard screenshots | Weekly | Conversion |
| **Process content** | Behind-the-scenes of how PMD works | Biweekly | Differentiation |
| **Engagement content** | Questions, polls, responses to industry news | As needed | Reach |

**Content rule:** Every piece answers one of three questions for the audience:
1. What should I know? (education)
2. What should I believe? (positioning)
3. What should I do? (action)

## Framework 2 — The Case Study Machine

Use when: converting client work into public content, building proof assets, course testimonials.

**Case study structure (PMD format):**
1. **The situation** — who the client is, what challenge they faced
2. **The diagnosis** — what PMD found that others missed
3. **The system** — what was built/deployed (keep proprietary details vague)
4. **The result** — specific, measurable outcomes (rankings, reviews, traffic, leads)
5. **The lesson** — one insight the audience can apply to their own practice

**Case study types:**
- SEO audit → implementation → ranking movement (SVS, BVI, DXUSS)
- GBP optimization → local search dominance
- Content system → consistent patient education without doctor time
- CRM automation → lead nurture without manual follow-up
- Dashboard → data-driven marketing decisions for the first time

**Distribution path:** Case study → LinkedIn post series → YouTube video → course module example → email nurture content

## Framework 3 — Course & Membership Narrative Architecture

Use when: designing course content, writing sales pages, onboarding messaging, positioning the offer.

**The authority course narrative arc:**

1. **The problem** — medical experts are invisible online despite being world-class at what they do. Marketing is complicated, expensive, and doesn't work without a system.
2. **The failed solutions** — hiring agencies (expensive, no control), DIY content (inconsistent, burns out), generic marketing courses (not healthcare, not AI-native)
3. **The insight** — the experts who win online don't just create content, they build *systems* that work without their constant attention
4. **The method** — the Paramount MD Authority Platform: a step-by-step system for building authority, attracting patients, and growing with AI
5. **The transformation** — from invisible expert → recognized authority with a marketing system that runs itself

**Course positioning statements:**
- "For medical experts who are excellent at their work but invisible online"
- "The only AI-powered marketing system built specifically for healthcare"
- "Stop paying for marketing you don't understand. Build a system you control."

**Membership value ladder:**
- Course (self-study) → Coaching (implementation support) → Done-with-you (systems built together) → Done-for-you (PMD agency)

## Framework 4 — LinkedIn Authority System

Use when: planning LinkedIn content, writing posts, building follower growth strategy.

**Profile optimization checklist:**
- Headline: Lead with who you help + specific outcome (not job title)
- Banner: Visual that communicates the authority platform concept
- About: Lead with the audience's problem, then Victor's unique position, then proof, then CTA
- Featured: Link to best case study, course, or lead magnet

**Post frameworks that build authority:**

*The Insight Post:* One counterintuitive thing I've learned about [topic] → explain → implication for audience

*The Case Study Post:* Here's how [client type] went from [before] to [after] in [timeframe] → 3-step breakdown → lesson

*The System Post:* The exact [system/framework] I use to [achieve outcome] → visual or numbered breakdown

*The Opinion Post:* Controversial take on [industry topic] → defend it → invite debate

*The Behind-the-Scenes Post:* What I'm building/doing right now → why → what I'm learning

**Posting cadence for authority:** 3–5x/week. Consistency > perfection. Volume builds the algorithm; quality builds trust.
