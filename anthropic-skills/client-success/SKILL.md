# Client Success System — Paramount MD

Client retention is PMD's most important revenue function. Acquiring a new client costs 5–10x more than retaining one. For a solo operator, every client lost is a significant revenue hit with no team to absorb it. Every system in this skill is designed to make clients successful, make that success visible, and make leaving unthinkable.

**Always read `/sessions/eloquent-pensive-bohr/mnt/CoWork OS/PMD-context.md` first** for current client roster and status.

## 1. Client Health Scoring

Run this mental model for every active client at least monthly. Score each dimension 1–5.

| Dimension | Green (4–5) | Yellow (2–3) | Red (1) |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|---------|
| **Results** | Measurable wins delivered this month | Some progress, nothing dramatic | No visible movement |
| **Communication** | Responsive, engaged, replies quickly | Slow to respond, occasional | Going dark, hard to reach |
| **Value perception** | Actively references our work | Neutral, not thinking about it | Questioning ROI |
| **Relationship depth** | Feels like a partner | Transactional | Purely contractual |
| **Expansion signals** | Asking about more services | Content with current scope | Shrinking conversations |

**Total score interpretation:**
- **20–25:** Healthy. Focus on expansion.
- **14–19:** Stable but needs attention. Identify the weakest dimension and address it.
- **8–13:** At risk. Intervene immediately — schedule a call, deliver a quick win.
- **Below 8:** Churn risk. Escalate to save conversation.

## 3. The QBR Framework

Quarterly Business Reviews should happen with every retainer client. They serve two purposes: demonstrate value (retention) and identify expansion opportunities (growth).

**QBR Agenda Template (45–60 min):**

**1. Open with wins (10 min)**
- Lead with 3 specific, measurable results from the quarter
- Frame in the client's language: not "we improved keyword rankings" but "your practice now appears on page 1 for [condition] in [city] — that's X new visitors per month"
- Show the dashboard. Let the data speak first.

**2. What we worked on (10 min)**
- Quick recap of deliverables completed
- Reinforce effort invested (clients forget what they're paying for)
- Acknowledge anything that didn't land as planned — own it, explain what changed

**3. What's next (15 min)**
- Present the Q+1 roadmap
- Prioritize 2–3 concrete deliverables
- Tie each deliverable to a business outcome they care about

**4. Expansion conversation (10 min)**
- Only raise if health score is 15+
- Introduce as "something I've been thinking about for you..." not a hard sell
- Frame as: "The work we've done on X is performing. The natural next step is Y — want me to put together a scope?"

**5. Questions + close (5 min)**
- Ask: "Is there anything we're not doing that you wish we were?"
- Confirm next check-in date
- Send recap within 24 hours

## 4. Expansion Playbook

Never wait for a client to ask for more. Identify the expansion signal and bring the idea to them.

**Expansion signals to watch for:**
- Client mentions a problem that's adjacent to current work ("we're not getting enough Google reviews")
- Client asks a question that's out of scope ("can you help with our social media?")
- Current work is clearly performing — client is happy and the logical next step is obvious
- Client's business is growing — new locations, new providers, new services

**PMD Expansion Matrix:**

| Current Service | Natural Upsell | Pitch Angle |
|-----------------|---------------|-------------|
| SEO audit (project) | Ongoing SEO retainer | "The fixes are live — now let's defend the gains and keep building" |
| SEO retainer | Content production | "Rankings are improving — content is what accelerates from here" |
| Marketing retainer | AI Systems & Automation | "We've built the marketing engine — now let's automate the operations underneath it" |
| Any retainer | Reporting dashboard | "Let's make your results visible in real time" |
| Agency client | Course referral | "If you ever want to learn how to run this yourself, I'm building a course for exactly that" |

## 5. Renewal Framing

Never let a contract renewal feel like a vendor negotiation. Frame it as a strategic conversation.

**Renewal conversation structure:**

1. **Lead with ROI, not price** — "Over the past [period], here's what we've delivered: [specific metrics]"
2. **Anchor on what's in motion** — "We're mid-way through [initiative] — stopping now means leaving [outcome] on the table"
3. **Present the next chapter, not a renewal** — "Here's what Q3/Year 2 looks like..." Make it forward-looking, not a renewal of the past
4. **Make the ask direct** — "I'd like to continue this partnership. Want me to send over the renewal terms?"

**Renewal timing:**
- Surface the conversation 30–45 days before contract end — never let it come as a surprise
- If client has been quiet, proactively schedule a value demonstration call before renewal conversation
- If there's been an issue, resolve it first — never try to renew on top of an unresolved problem

## 6. Churn Prevention — Early Warning Response

If a client shows two or more yellow signals OR any red signal, intervene immediately.

**Intervention playbook:**

**Step 1: Diagnose before acting**
- Is this about results (not seeing enough value)?
- Is this about communication (feeling ignored or out of the loop)?
- Is this about budget (external financial pressure)?
- Is this about expectations (they expected something different)?

**Step 2: Act based on root cause**

| Root cause | Response |
|------------|----------|
| Results gap | Deliver a quick win immediately. Audit what's working, double down. Schedule a call to present it. |
| Communication gap | Increase touch frequency. Proactive update within 24 hours. Add a standing monthly check-in. |
| Budget pressure | Offer to right-size the scope rather than lose the relationship entirely. A smaller retainer beats $0. |
| Expectation mismatch | Direct conversation. "I want to make sure we're aligned on what success looks like. Can we realign?" |

**Step 3: The save conversation**
- Lead with: "I want to make sure you're getting the value you expected from this partnership."
- Do not lead with defending your work — listen first.
- Present a concrete plan to close the gap.
- Give them a 30-day "prove it" mini-sprint if needed.

## 7. Client Communication Cadence

For every retainer client, maintain this minimum touch frequency:

| Touch type | Frequency | Format |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Quick update / progress note | Weekly | Email or Slack message — 3 sentences max |
| Dashboard review | Monthly | Loom walkthrough or live call |
| QBR | Quarterly | Live call, 45–60 min |
| Renewal / expansion conversation | As needed | Live call, prepare with QBR data |

**Communication rule:** Never let a client go more than 7 days without hearing from PMD. Silence = they think nothing is happening.

## 8. Case Study Extraction

Every successful client outcome is a marketing asset. After any significant win:

1. Document: What was the situation before? What did we do? What were the measurable results?
2. Get permission: Ask the client if they're comfortable being referenced (even anonymously)
3. Format for distribution: Short form (LinkedIn post / email), long form (case study page), and internal (for proposals)
4. Route to `content-repurposing` for multi-platform distribution

**Case study trigger moments:** Rankings breakthrough, traffic milestone, review count milestone, lead volume increase, system launch, Phase completion with measurable outcome.
