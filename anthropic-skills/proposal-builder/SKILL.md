# Proposal Builder — Paramount MD

PMD's AI Systems engagements are high-ticket and relationship-dependent. A well-crafted proposal does three things: demonstrates that you understand the client's problem better than they do, shows the path from where they are to where they want to be, and makes the decision to say yes easy.

A weak proposal loses deals that the discovery call won. A strong proposal closes deals while Victor is still on the phone.

**Always read `/sessions/eloquent-pensive-bohr/mnt/CoWork OS/PMD-context.md` first** — engagement tiers, positioning, and current prospect context all inform the proposal.

---

## 1. Pre-Proposal Checklist

Before writing anything, confirm you have answers to:

- [ ] What specific problem(s) are they trying to solve?
- [ ] What does success look like to them in 90 days? 1 year?
- [ ] What have they already tried? Why didn't it work?
- [ ] Who will be the internal owner/champion?
- [ ] What's their rough budget range? (If unknown, build tiered options)
- [ ] What's their timeline / urgency?
- [ ] Are there any compliance or technical constraints? (HIPAA, existing systems, IT restrictions)
- [ ] Is this Tier A (PMD manages ongoing) or Tier B (PMD builds, they take over)?

Missing answers = schedule a discovery call before writing. Don't propose blind.

## 2. Proposal Structure

Every PMD proposal follows this structure. Adjust length based on engagement size.

---

### Section 1: The Situation (1 page)

Restate the client's situation in your own words — better than they said it. This is the most important section. If they read this and think "yes, exactly," you've already won.

**Template:**
> "[Client Name] is facing [specific challenge]. Currently, [describe the manual/broken/inefficient state]. This creates [concrete pain: time cost, revenue loss, missed opportunities, competitive disadvantage]. The goal is to [desired outcome] — but without the right systems in place, that's not achievable at the pace or scale [Client Name] needs.

This section should feel like a mirror. Not a sales pitch — a diagnosis.

---

### Section 2: The Approach (1–2 pages)

Describe how PMD will solve it. Be specific about methodology, not just deliverables.

**Structure:**
1. **Phase 1 — Discovery & Architecture** (always included): Deep-dive into their current systems, map the workflow, identify automation opportunities, design the system architecture
2. **Phase 2 — Build**: Construct the Make.com scenarios, GHL automations, AI agent workflows
3. **Phase 3 — Test & Refine**: QA testing with real data, edge case handling, compliance review
4. **Phase 4 — Launch & Stabilize**: Go-live, monitoring, issue resolution in the first 30 days
5. **Ongoing — Manage or Transition** (Tier A or B): Either PMD runs it, or PMD trains and hands off

Describe each phase in 2–4 sentences. Enough detail to be credible, not so much that it becomes a technical spec.

---

### Section 3: What You Get (deliverables list)

Concrete, specific, and client-language. Not "system architecture documentation" but "a plain-English guide showing exactly how your system works, what to do if something breaks, and how to make changes."

**Format each deliverable as:**
- **[Deliverable name]:** [1 sentence — what it is and why it matters to them]

**Standard deliverables for AI Systems engagements:**
- **System Architecture Design:** A complete map of every automation, trigger, and AI component in your new system
- **Make.com Scenarios:** [X] built and tested automation workflows handling [specific functions]
- **GHL Workflow Automations:** [X] automated workflows for [specific functions — appointments, follow-up, etc.]
- **AI Agent Configuration:** Custom Claude-powered agents for [specific jobs — response drafting, content generation, data analysis]
- **Integration Setup:** Connections between [list their existing platforms] and the new system
- **System Documentation:** Operating guide, troubleshooting guide, and change log
- **Training Session:** [For Tier B only] Live walkthrough with your team — recorded for reference
- **30-Day Support Window:** Any issues in the first month are fixed at no additional charge

---

### Section 4: Investment (the pricing section)

Always present two options. A single price gives the client a yes/no decision. Two options give them a choice between two yeses.

**Tiered option format:**

**Option A — [Build + Manage / "Mothership"]**
> PMD builds the complete system and continues to operate, monitor, and optimize it for you on an ongoing basis. You never have to touch it — it just runs.

| | |
|--|--|
| **Build fee (one-time):** | $[X] |
| **Monthly management retainer:** | $[X]/month |
| **Minimum commitment:** | 6 months |
| **What's included monthly:** | System monitoring, optimization, monthly performance report, priority support |

**Option B — [Build + Transition]**
> PMD builds the complete system, trains your team to operate it, and provides 90 days of check-ins and support as you take ownership.

| | |
|--|--|
| **Build fee (one-time):** | $[X] |
| **90-day support retainer:** | $[X]/month × 3 |
| **Training:** | Included — 2 live sessions, recorded |
| **After 90 days:** | Optional ongoing support at $[X]/month |

**Pricing guidance by complexity:**

| Complexity | Build Fee Range | Monthly Management |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Simple (1–2 automations) | $1,500–$3,500 | $500–$800/mo |
| Medium (3–5 integrations, AI-enhanced) | $4,000–$8,000 | $800–$1,500/mo |
| Complex (full digitization, multi-agent) | $10,000–$25,000+ | $1,500–$3,000/mo |

Always anchor pricing to value, not hours. The conversation is: "This automation will save your team X hours per week. At [$Y/hour], that's [$Z/month] in recovered capacity — before accounting for revenue from better follow-up systems."

---

### Section 5: Timeline

Set clear expectations. Clients often underestimate how long good systems take to build.

**Standard timeline template:**

| Phase | Duration | What happens |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Discovery & Architecture | Week 1–2 | Deep-dive sessions, workflow mapping, system design |
| Build | Week 3–5 | Make.com scenarios, GHL workflows, AI agent config |
| Test & Refine | Week 6 | Testing with real data, edge cases, compliance check |
| Launch | Week 7 | Go-live, monitoring, issue resolution |
| Stabilize | Week 8 | Final adjustments, documentation delivery |
| Training (Tier B) | Week 8–9 | Live training sessions, recorded walkthroughs |

Total: approximately 8–10 weeks from signed agreement to full launch.

---

### Section 6: Why PMD

One page maximum. This is not a list of credentials — it's a proof statement.

**Template:**
> We don't just design systems — we run them. Everything in this proposal is based on systems we've already built and operate for healthcare clients. We understand the compliance landscape (HIPAA, patient data, PHI), the workflow realities of medical practices, and how to build automations that hold up under real-world pressure.
>
> PMD sits at the intersection of three disciplines that rarely overlap: healthcare domain expertise, AI-native automation, and marketing systems. That combination is what makes our systems different — they're not generic automation templates retrofitted for healthcare. They're built from scratch around how healthcare organizations actually work.
>
> [If applicable: "We recently completed a similar engagement for [anonymized client type] — the result was [specific outcome]."]

---

### Section 7: Terms & Next Steps

Keep it clean. The legal detail goes in the contract — the proposal closes the deal.

**Standard terms:**
- **Deposit:** 50% of build fee due upon signing to begin work
- **Remainder:** 50% due upon system launch / delivery
- **Monthly retainer:** Billed on the 1st of each month, 30-day payment terms
- **Intellectual property:** Systems, prompts, and documentation remain PMD's property; client receives a perpetual license to use them as long as the relationship is active (adjust for Tier B — full ownership transferred upon final payment)
- **HIPAA:** BAA (Business Associate Agreement) required before accessing any patient data

**Next steps section:**
> To move forward:
> 1. Review this proposal and let me know if you have questions
> 2. I'll send a formal agreement for signature
> 3. Upon signed agreement and deposit receipt, we schedule the Discovery kickoff call
> 4. Work begins within [X] business days

**Always end with a specific ask:** "I'd like to get on a 30-minute call to walk through this together and answer any questions. Are you available [Tuesday or Wednesday] this week?"

## 3. Discovery Call Prep

Use this before the discovery call to structure the conversation and ensure the proposal writes itself afterward.

**Pre-call research (use `sales:account-research`):**
- What does the organization do, how big are they, what's their tech stack?
- What's their current online presence / marketing maturity?
- Any recent news, expansions, or visible operational pain points?

**Call agenda (45–60 min):**
1. **[5 min]** Rapport + context — why they reached out, what prompted the conversation
2. **[20 min]** Discovery — walk through the 5-area discovery protocol from `ai-workflow-designer`
3. **[10 min]** Vision — "What would this look like if it worked perfectly?"
4. **[5 min]** Constraints — budget range, timeline, internal considerations
5. **[5 min]** Next steps — "I'll put together a proposal based on what we've discussed. I'll have it to you by [date]."
6. **[5 min]** Any questions

**Post-call:** Send a 3-bullet summary email within 2 hours. Confirm your understanding, state next step and timeline. This keeps momentum and signals professionalism.

## 4. Objection Handling

**"It's too expensive."**
Reframe to value: "I understand — let me show you how we think about the ROI. Your team currently spends [X hours] per week on [manual process]. At [hourly rate], that's [$Y/month] in labor. This system eliminates that. You're not paying for automation — you're buying back time."

**"We want to do it ourselves."**
Respect the instinct, then: "That's Option B — we build it, train your team, and hand it off. You get the full benefit of what we've built without the ongoing dependency. Want me to put together what that looks like?"

**"We need to think about it."**
Ask: "Of course. What's the main thing you're trying to get clarity on? I want to make sure the proposal answers the right questions." Then address it. A vague "think about it" usually means a specific unresolved concern.

**"We already tried something like this."**
Ask: "What happened?" Listen carefully — this tells you exactly what to avoid and what to position against. "What we do differently is [specific thing that addresses what failed]."

## 5. Proposal Output Format

When generating a proposal from this skill, produce:

1. **Full proposal document** — formatted for sharing (route to `docx` skill for Word document output)
2. **Executive summary** — 1-page version for quick sharing with additional stakeholders
3. **Follow-up email** — the email to send with the proposal attachment
4. **Objection prep card** — the 3 most likely objections for this specific deal and the response for each

Flag any gaps in discovery information that would strengthen the proposal if answered before sending.
